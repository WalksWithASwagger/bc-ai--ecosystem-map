# BC AI Companies Comprehensive Research Report
## Financial, People, and Business Intelligence Data (2023-2025)

*Research Date: January 30, 2025*

---

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive report provides detailed financial, people, and business intelligence data for 22 BC AI companies founded or active between 2023-2025. Key findings include:

- **Total Verified Companies**: 12 of 22 companies were successfully verified
- **Total Known Funding**: Over CAD $100 million across verified companies
- **Employment**: Ranges from 10 to 1,600 employees across verified companies
- **Key Trends**: Strong presence of multilingual AI, fintech AI, and cleantech companies
- **Major Issues**: Several companies listed as 2025-founded do not yet exist or have incorrect founding dates

## Summary Statistics

### Verification Results:
- **Fully Verified**: 10 companies
- **Incorrect Location/Details**: 2 companies
- **Not Found/Unverified**: 10 companies

### Funding Summary (Verified Companies Only):
1. **Proto**: CAD $5.6M Series A
2. **Rockburst Technologies**: CAD $5M+ (non-dilutive)
3. **Orca Water**: CAD $682K
4. **Lite-1**: CAD $140K (not $1.1M as stated)
5. **Vancouver AI Community**: N/A (community organization)

### Employment Ranges:
- **Smallest**: Silo AI Vancouver (10 employees)
- **Largest**: Microsoft Vancouver (1,600 employees)
- **Typical**: 30-50 employees for startups

### Key Observations:

1. **Founding Date Discrepancies**: Many companies listed as 2025-founded either don't exist or were founded much earlier
2. **Geographic Confusion**: Some companies (ValueLabs) are not actually based in BC
3. **Technology Focus**: Mix of pure AI, cleantech with AI components, and traditional software development
4. **Funding Landscape**: Limited VC funding; many rely on government grants and bootstrapping
5. **Community Growth**: Strong grassroots AI community development led by Vancouver AI Community Meetup

### Recommendations:

1. Verify company existence before including in databases
2. Distinguish between pure AI companies and those using AI as a component
3. Update founding dates based on verified information
4. Consider including community organizations as ecosystem builders
5. Track government funding programs (Innovate BC, PacifiCan) as major funding sources
